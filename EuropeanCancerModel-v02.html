TreatmentResponse
Model-Generated Definition:
A kind of thing that meets all of the following conditions:
  • treatmentResponseEvaluatedThroughEvidence exactly one occurrence of Evidence
  • treatmentResponseisEvaluatedDuringVisit exactly one occurrence of Visit
This concept is the domain of treatmentResponseEvaluatedThroughEvidence, treatmentResponsehasResponsetype, and treatmentResponseisEvaluatedDuringVisit
This concept is the range of treatmentCanHaveTreatmentResponse
Definition: Represents the a standard way to measure how well a cancer patient responds to a single Treatment. It is based on whether tumours shrink, stay the same, or get bigger.

It could seem like a repetition of the Disease Status, but it doesn’t. It is important to have Treatment Response and Disease Status separated because they have different purposes. The Treatment Response is used to evaluate the effects of the Treatment while Disease Status is to represent the evolution of the Cancer Condition over the time.

TumorGrade
Model-Generated Definition:
A kind of thing that tumorGradeIsDefindedByBiopsy exactly one occurrence of Biopsy
This concept is the domain of tumorGradeIsDefindedByBiopsy
This concept is the range of cancerConditionHasTumorGrade
Definition: Describes a tumour in terms of how abnormal the tumour cells are. This is compared to normal cells. It also describes how abnormal the tissues look under a microscope. It describes the biological aggressiveness of the tumour. The grade provides some idea of how the cancer might behave. A low-grade cancer is likely to grow more slowly and be less likely to spread than a high grade one.
It never changes even if the Disease Status evolves in time.
